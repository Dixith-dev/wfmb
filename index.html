What is Art Therapy?

Art therapy is a form of psychotherapy that uses art media as its primary mode of communication. It combines traditional psychotherapeutic theories with an understanding of the psychological aspects of the creative process.

Clients don't need to have any art experience or skill to benefit from art therapy. The focus is on the process of creation rather than the final product.

Benefits of Art Therapy

Stress Reduction

Art making can lower stress levels and promote relaxation through focused creative activity.

Emotional Expression

Provides a non-verbal outlet for expressing complex emotions that may be difficult to articulate.

Self-Discovery

Helps individuals gain insight into their feelings, behaviors, and thought patterns.

Art Therapy Techniques

Mandala Drawing

Creating circular designs to promote focus, relaxation, and self-expression.

Collage Making

Combining images and materials to explore themes and express emotions.
